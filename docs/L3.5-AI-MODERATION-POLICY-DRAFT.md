## 1. Output - Repository audit summary

Findings from the implementation (see Output 5 for the consistency review):

**Implemented and relied on by this Policy:**
- **Photo moderation (provider-pluggable).** A single automated entry point assesses each uploaded photo through a configurable, ordered chain of providers. Adapters exist for **OpenAI moderation** (omni-moderation, REST, requires an API key), **AWS Rekognition** (moderation labels and face detection), and **Azure Content Safety**. Providers return scores for adult/sexual content, violence, and minor-risk, plus labels and a confidence value. **Which provider is active depends on configuration; if none is configured, automated photo moderation does not run and content relies on user reporting and human review.**
- **Decision engine.** Provider scores are mapped to a severity (low to critical) and an action, using **configurable thresholds** (for example, a minor-risk threshold and a policy-critical confidence). A minor-risk signal is treated as critical (see the Child Safety Policy, `/legal/child-safety`).
- **Verification risk engine.** A separate engine weighs normalised verification signals into a risk band (low, medium, high, or critical); a critical band blocks automatic verification and forces manual review. It outputs only a band and normalised signal names; raw provider scores never enter or leave it.
- **Verification providers.** AWS Face Liveness and AWS Rekognition (photo verification) and Stripe Identity (identity verification).
- **Human review and control.** A moderation queue with severity-based prioritisation; human override; human-only permanent bans; appeals; append-only, PII-stripped audit records; and configuration-driven rollout and kill-switch controls for the face-verification system.

**Not implemented (implementation gaps - this Policy must not claim them):**
- **No automated spam classifier.** Spam is handled through user reports and human review.
- **No automated fake-profile classifier.** Fake or impersonating profiles are handled through user reports and human review.
- **No automated self-harm enforcement.** A provider may surface a self-harm label, but self-harm is not a first-class enforced moderation signal.
- **No automated profile-text or message scanning.** Automated moderation applies to photos only; text and messages rely on user reporting and human review.
- **No dedicated bias-monitoring or fairness-auditing system.** Thresholds and calibration are configurable and reviewed, but there is no formal automated bias-monitoring pipeline.
- **No AI-regime certification.** Tirvea is not certified under the EU AI Act or any equivalent regime.

Each gap is reflected in the relevant section and carries a **[Legal review required before publication.]** flag where a legal obligation may exist that the implementation does not yet meet.

## 4. Output - Complete AI Moderation Policy

> **Reading note.** Each operative section states its **Purpose**, **Scope**, **Implementation status**, **Trigger**, **Possible evidence**, **Decision maker**, **Possible outcomes**, and **Appeal availability**. The governing principles run throughout: **AI assists moderation and does not replace human judgment; AI outputs are treated as evidence and recommendations, not final decisions; AI recommendations are reviewed by a person where the decision is consequential; a permanent ban is never issued solely by AI; human reviewers may override AI; confidence thresholds are configurable; and AI systems are continuously evaluated.**

**Purpose.** To govern every use of artificial intelligence and automated decision-support in moderation on Tirvea: how AI is used, where human review is required, what AI may and may not decide, and the transparency, rights, and safeguards that apply.

**Scope.** All automated moderation and decision-support systems.

**Implementation status.** Framework section.

**Trigger.** Not applicable.

**Possible evidence.** Not applicable.

**Decision maker.** WiseWave Limited, through its Trust & Safety and AI-governance functions.

**Possible outcomes.** A clear, accountable framework for AI-assisted moderation.

**Appeal availability.** Moderation decisions are appealable under the Appeals Policy (`/legal/appeals`).

**Purpose.** To state what this Policy covers.

**Implementation status.** Framework section.

### 3. Definitions

**Purpose.** To define AI terms used here. Conduct rules are in the Community Guidelines (`/legal/community-guidelines`); enforcement states in the Account Suspension Policy (`/legal/account-suspension`); data terms in the Privacy Policy (`/legal/privacy`).

- **"AI system"** - an automated system that classifies, scores, or flags content or signals to assist a moderation or safety decision.
- **"Provider"** - a third-party or internal adapter that returns a moderation or verification result.
- **"Score" / "confidence"** - a numeric output indicating the strength of a signal.
- **"Risk band"** - a normalised classification (low, medium, high, critical) produced by the verification risk engine.
- **"Human review"** - assessment or confirmation of a decision by a trained person.

**Implementation status / trigger / evidence / decision maker / outcomes / appeal.** Not applicable (definitional).

### 4. AI Governance Principles

**Purpose.** To state the principles that constrain every AI system.

**Scope.** All AI-assisted moderation.

**Implementation status.** Implemented as operating principles.

**Trigger.** Not applicable.

**Possible evidence.** Not applicable.
