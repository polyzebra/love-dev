## 3. Output — Complete Community Guidelines

> **Reading note.** Each conduct section states its **Purpose**, the **Expected behaviour**, the **Prohibited behaviour**, and the **Possible enforcement**. Enforcement actions are drawn from the single ladder described in §§41–44; naming them per section is indicative, not a fixed tariff — actual outcomes depend on severity, intent, context, and history.

### 1. Welcome

**Purpose.** Tirvea is an adults-only (18+) dating platform operated by WiseWave Limited. These Community Guidelines welcome you and set out how we expect members to treat one another so that Tirvea stays a safe, respectful, and genuine place to meet people.

**Expected behaviour.** Engage honestly, treat others with respect, and help keep the community safe by following these Guidelines and reporting problems.

**Prohibited behaviour.** Using Tirvea in ways that harm, deceive, or endanger other members, as described throughout this document.

**Possible enforcement.** The full range in §§41–44, up to permanent removal, applies to serious or repeated breaches.

### 2. Purpose

**Purpose.** To define one clear behavioural standard for everyone on Tirvea, to explain how we make and enforce moderation decisions, and to describe how our Trust & Safety function protects members. These Guidelines work alongside the Terms of Service (`/legal/terms`), which govern your contract with WiseWave Limited, and the Acceptable Use Policy (`/legal/acceptable-use`), which sets out the formal prohibitions.

**Expected behaviour.** Read these Guidelines, understand what is expected, and apply them in your profile, your messages, and your interactions.

**Prohibited behaviour.** Treating the Guidelines as optional, or attempting to evade them.

**Possible enforcement.** Any enforcement action in §§41–44, proportionate to the conduct.

### 3. Community Values

**Purpose.** Our rules flow from four values: **Respect** (treat others as you would wish to be treated), **Authenticity** (be genuinely yourself), **Safety** (protect yourself and others), and **Integrity** (act honestly and lawfully). Where a situation is not expressly covered by a rule, these values guide how we interpret it.

**Expected behaviour.** Let these values shape your conduct even where no specific rule applies.

**Prohibited behaviour.** Conduct that plainly offends these values, even if novel, may be actioned under the value it breaches.

**Possible enforcement.** Proportionate action under §§41–44.

### 4. Respect

**Purpose.** To ensure every member is treated with dignity, regardless of background or identity.

**Expected behaviour.** Communicate courteously; accept that not everyone will be interested in you; disengage gracefully; respect boundaries and a "no".

**Prohibited behaviour.** Insults, degrading remarks, contempt for a person's identity, or persistent unwanted contact. Targeted abuse is addressed in §§13–16.

**Possible enforcement.** Warning, temporary restriction, or removal depending on severity (§§42–44).

### 5. Authenticity

**Purpose.** Tirvea is built on real people being genuinely themselves; authenticity is the foundation of trust between members.

**Expected behaviour.** Represent yourself truthfully — your identity, your photos, your intentions.

**Prohibited behaviour.** Impersonation, fake profiles, misleading photos, or misrepresenting who you are or what you want (see §§7–8, 24–25).

**Possible enforcement.** Profile correction, identity or photo review, restriction, or removal (§§41–44).

### 6. Identity Verification

**Purpose.** Verification helps confirm that members are real people, reducing impersonation and fraud. How it works and what we store is set out in the Identity Verification Policy (`/legal/identity-verification`) and Photo Verification Policy (`/legal/photo-verification`); the associated data is governed by the Privacy Policy (`/legal/privacy`).

**Expected behaviour.** Complete verification honestly when asked, using your own genuine identity and likeness.

**Prohibited behaviour.** Submitting false, forged, or third-party identity information, or attempting to defeat verification checks.

**Possible enforcement.** Failed or fraudulent verification may lead to `PHOTO_REVIEW_REQUIRED` status, restriction, suspension, or removal (§§43–44), and may be referred to authorities (§47).

### 7. Profile Accuracy

**Purpose.** Accurate profiles let members make informed choices and keep the community genuine.

**Expected behaviour.** Keep your profile truthful and current — your age (18+), photos, and material details.

**Prohibited behaviour.** Materially false or misleading profile information, including age misrepresentation (see §§20–21 for age).

**Possible enforcement.** Required correction, reduced visibility pending review, restriction, or removal (§§42–44).

### 8. Photos

**Purpose.** Photos should genuinely represent you and be appropriate for an adult dating community.

**Expected behaviour.** Use recent photos of yourself; ensure you have the right to share any image; respect others' image rights.

**Prohibited behaviour.** Photos that are not of you, stolen or copyrighted images used without right, images of other identifiable people without their consent, or prohibited content (see §§18–19, 29). Uploads may be subject to photo review.

**Possible enforcement.** Photo removal, `PHOTO_REVIEW_REQUIRED` status, restriction, or removal (§§40–44).

### 9. Messaging

**Purpose.** Messaging is for genuine, respectful connection between members.

**Expected behaviour.** Message courteously; take "no" for an answer; keep conversations consensual and lawful.

**Prohibited behaviour.** Harassment, threats, hate, unsolicited explicit content, spam, solicitation, or scams via messages (see §§13–27). The formal prohibitions are in the Acceptable Use Policy (`/legal/acceptable-use`).

**Possible enforcement.** Message removal, feature limits (`LIMITED` status removes outbound messaging), suspension, or removal (§§43–44).

### 10. Consent

**Purpose.** Consent is central to healthy interaction on a dating platform, online and offline.

**Expected behaviour.** Seek and respect consent before sending intimate content or escalating contact; stop when asked; honour blocks and unmatches.

**Prohibited behaviour.** Ignoring stated boundaries, pressuring or coercing others, sending unsolicited explicit images (§19), or any non-consensual conduct.

**Possible enforcement.** Restriction, suspension, or removal (§§43–44); conduct that may be criminal is referred to authorities (§47).

### 11. Dating Safety

**Purpose.** To help you stay safe as you get to know people on Tirvea. Additional guidance is in the Trust & Safety Policy (`/legal/trust-safety`) and Safety Centre.

**Expected behaviour.** Protect your personal information; be cautious with people you have not met; trust your instincts; report anything that feels wrong (§38). Be alert to romance scams, requests for money, and pressure to move off-platform (§§22–23).

**Prohibited behaviour.** Exploiting the trust of other members, or using the Service to set up harm.

**Possible enforcement.** The full ladder (§§41–44), and referral to authorities where appropriate (§47).

### 12. Offline Meetings

**Purpose.** Meeting in person is a natural step; these Guidelines help you do so safely.

**Expected behaviour.** Meet first in public; tell someone you trust where you will be; arrange your own transport; do not share your home address early; stop if you feel unsafe. Never send money to someone you have not met (§22).

**Prohibited behaviour.** Coercing a meeting, misrepresenting who will attend, or using a meeting to defraud, harm, or exploit another member.

**Possible enforcement.** Removal and referral to authorities for conduct that endangers others (§§44, 47).

### 13. Harassment

**Purpose.** Everyone deserves to use Tirvea free from harassment.

**Expected behaviour.** Disengage respectfully; use block and report instead of retaliating.

**Prohibited behaviour.** Repeated unwanted contact, sexual harassment, intimidation, stalking behaviours, or coordinated targeting of a member.

**Possible enforcement.** Warning through to permanent removal depending on severity (§§42–44); referral to authorities where conduct may be criminal (§47).

### 14. Bullying

**Purpose.** To protect members from demeaning and hostile behaviour.

**Expected behaviour.** Treat others with basic decency even in disagreement or rejection.

**Prohibited behaviour.** Insults, humiliation, body-shaming, mocking, or encouraging others to pile on against a member.

**Possible enforcement.** Warning, restriction, or removal (§§42–44).

### 15. Threats

**Purpose.** Threats have no place on Tirvea and can be criminal.

**Expected behaviour.** Keep all interactions non-violent and lawful; report any threat you receive (§§38–39).

**Prohibited behaviour.** Threats of violence, harm, or exposure (including blackmail and sextortion, §22) against any person.

**Possible enforcement.** Immediate suspension or removal (§§43–44) and referral to law enforcement (§47); credible threats may trigger emergency escalation (§39).

### 16. Hate Speech

**Purpose.** Tirvea is for everyone; hate has no place here.

**Expected behaviour.** Respect the dignity of all members regardless of protected characteristics.

**Prohibited behaviour.** Attacks, slurs, dehumanisation, or incitement of hatred or violence based on race, ethnicity, national origin, religion, disability, sex, gender identity, sexual orientation, or other protected characteristic.

**Possible enforcement.** Content removal, restriction, or removal (§§42–44); illegal hate speech is handled under §29 and referred where required (§47).

### 17. Violence

**Purpose.** To keep the community free from violent content and incitement.

**Expected behaviour.** Keep content non-violent; report threats or incitement.

**Prohibited behaviour.** Threatening, glorifying, or inciting violence; sharing graphic violent content; organising harm.

**Possible enforcement.** Removal of content, suspension, or permanent removal (§§43–44); referral to authorities for credible threats (§47).

### 18. Sexual Content

**Purpose.** Tirvea is an adult platform, but it is a dating service, not an adult-content service; this section sets the boundary. The formal limits are in the Acceptable Use Policy (`/legal/acceptable-use`).

**Expected behaviour.** Keep profile content appropriate for a dating context; keep intimate exchanges private, consensual, and between adults.

**Prohibited behaviour.** Pornographic profile content, sexual solicitation or prostitution, commercial sexual services, and any sexual content involving minors (absolute prohibition, §§20–21).

**Possible enforcement.** Content removal, restriction, suspension, or removal (§§42–44).

### 19. Explicit Images

**Purpose.** To prevent non-consensual and unwanted explicit imagery.

**Expected behaviour.** Never send explicit images without clear, prior consent; respect that consent can be withdrawn.

**Prohibited behaviour.** Unsolicited explicit images; sharing intimate images of others without consent ("image-based abuse"); threatening to share such images (§22). Any explicit image of a minor is prohibited absolutely and handled under §21.

**Possible enforcement.** Immediate removal and, for non-consensual or abusive imagery, suspension or removal (§§43–44) and referral to authorities (§47).

### 20. Minors

**Purpose.** Tirvea is strictly for adults aged 18 or over. No exceptions.

**Expected behaviour.** Only use Tirvea if you are 18 or older; report any profile you believe belongs to a minor (§§38–39).

**Prohibited behaviour.** Access or use by anyone under 18; any misrepresentation of age; any content sexualising a minor (§21).

**Possible enforcement.** Accounts believed to belong to minors are removed; see the Child Safety Policy (`/legal/child-safety`). Age assurance is described in the Privacy Policy (`/legal/privacy`) and Child Safety Policy.

### 21. Child Safety

**Purpose.** We operate a **zero-tolerance** policy for any content or conduct that sexualises, exploits, or endangers a child. This section reflects that absolute stance; full detail is in the Child Safety Policy (`/legal/child-safety`).

**Expected behaviour.** Report any suspected child-safety concern immediately (§39); never engage with such content beyond reporting it.

**Prohibited behaviour (absolute):** child sexual abuse material (CSAM); grooming or attempting to contact or exploit a minor; sexualisation of minors; any facilitation of child exploitation. There is no context in which this is permitted.

**Possible enforcement.** Immediate permanent removal (§44), preservation of relevant evidence, mandatory reporting to the appropriate authorities and hotlines, and full cooperation with law enforcement (§47). **[Legal review required before publication — confirm mandatory-reporting obligations and the designated reporting bodies (for example, national hotlines and, where applicable, NCMEC) for the launch jurisdictions.]**

### 22. Scams

**Purpose.** To protect members from romance scams and financial exploitation, a primary safety risk on dating platforms. We operate automated and human systems to detect scam patterns (§40).

**Expected behaviour.** Never send money, gift cards, or crypto to someone you met on Tirvea; be wary of fast-moving affection, sob stories, investment "opportunities", and pressure to move off-platform; report suspected scams (§38).

**Prohibited behaviour.** Romance scams; advance-fee and investment fraud; blackmail, sextortion, and extortion; catfishing to deceive or defraud; any scheme to obtain money or benefit by deception.

**Possible enforcement.** Suspension or permanent removal (§§43–44), and referral to authorities (§47). We may issue in-product safety notices to members who appear to be targeted.

### 23. Fraud

**Purpose.** To keep Tirvea free from fraudulent and deceptive activity. Our fraud-detection and risk-scoring systems are described in §40 and the Privacy Policy (`/legal/privacy`).

**Expected behaviour.** Use Tirvea genuinely and honestly.

**Prohibited behaviour.** Payment fraud, chargeback abuse, account-takeover attempts, use of stolen identities or payment methods, and evasion of enforcement (§46).

**Possible enforcement.** Restriction, suspension, or permanent removal (§§43–44); referral to authorities and payment partners (§47).

### 24. Impersonation

**Purpose.** To ensure members are who they claim to be.

**Expected behaviour.** Represent only yourself; use your own identity and images.

**Prohibited behaviour.** Impersonating another real person, a public figure, or WiseWave Limited or its staff; creating profiles designed to deceive about identity.

**Possible enforcement.** Identity/photo review, suspension, or removal (§§43–44).

### 25. Fake Profiles

**Purpose.** Fake profiles undermine trust and are often used for scams.

**Expected behaviour.** Maintain a single, genuine profile that represents you (see the one-account rule in the Terms, `/legal/terms`).

**Prohibited behaviour.** Fictitious profiles, bots, bulk or duplicate accounts, and profiles created to evade enforcement. We use duplicate-account detection (§40).

**Possible enforcement.** Removal of the offending accounts and, for evasion, action against linked accounts (§46).

### 26. Spam

**Purpose.** To keep interactions genuine and free from unwanted bulk activity.

**Expected behaviour.** Message people because you are genuinely interested, not to broadcast.

**Prohibited behaviour.** Bulk or repetitive messaging, automated messaging, and unsolicited promotional content (§27).

**Possible enforcement.** Rate limits, feature restriction (`LIMITED`), suspension, or removal (§§43–44).

### 27. Commercial Promotion

**Purpose.** Tirvea is for dating, not for advertising or selling.

**Expected behaviour.** Keep your use personal and non-commercial.

**Prohibited behaviour.** Advertising, promotion, recruitment, solicitation, sex work, or directing members to commercial or paid services. The formal prohibition is in the Acceptable Use Policy (`/legal/acceptable-use`).

**Possible enforcement.** Content removal, restriction, or removal (§§42–44).

### 28. External Links

**Purpose.** To protect members from off-platform harm, phishing, and scams.

**Expected behaviour.** Be cautious about links; do not follow links from people you do not trust; report suspicious links (§38).

**Prohibited behaviour.** Sharing malicious, phishing, fraudulent, or prohibited links, or using links to move members to unsafe off-platform environments (a common scam tactic, §22).

**Possible enforcement.** Link/content removal, restriction, suspension, or removal (§§42–44).

### 29. Illegal Content

**Purpose.** To keep the Service free of unlawful content and to meet our obligations for illegal content under applicable law, including the Digital Services Act.

**Expected behaviour.** Keep all content lawful; report suspected illegal content (§38), which we treat under our notice-and-action process (§40).

**Prohibited behaviour.** Any content that is illegal under applicable law, including CSAM (§21), illegal hate speech (§16), terrorist content, and unlawful sale of regulated goods (§§30–31).

**Possible enforcement.** Removal, restriction, suspension, or permanent removal (§§42–44); preservation and referral where required (§47). **[Legal review required before publication — confirm illegal-content categories and notice-and-action handling against the Digital Services Act and Irish implementing law.]**

### 30. Weapons

**Purpose.** To prohibit the unlawful trade and promotion of weapons through Tirvea.

**Expected behaviour.** Keep the Service free of weapons content.

**Prohibited behaviour.** Offering, selling, or trafficking firearms, ammunition, explosives, or other regulated weapons; promoting or instructing their unlawful use.

**Possible enforcement.** Content removal, suspension, or permanent removal (§§43–44), and referral to authorities (§47).

### 31. Drugs

**Purpose.** To prohibit the unlawful trade and promotion of controlled substances.

**Expected behaviour.** Keep the Service free of illegal-drug content.

**Prohibited behaviour.** Offering, selling, or trafficking illegal drugs or controlled substances, or facilitating their supply.

**Possible enforcement.** Content removal, suspension, or permanent removal (§§43–44), and referral to authorities (§47).

### 32. Self-Harm

**Purpose.** To respond with care to members in distress while preventing content that promotes harm.

**Expected behaviour.** If you are struggling, reach out to a trusted person or a crisis service. If you are worried about another member, report it (§38) so we can direct support resources.

**Prohibited behaviour.** Content that encourages, promotes, or provides instructions for suicide or self-harm, or that pressures another member toward self-harm.

**Possible enforcement.** Removal of harmful content and, where appropriate, provision of supportive resources rather than punitive action for those in distress; restriction or removal for those who promote harm to others (§§42–44). **[Legal review required before publication — confirm the signposting of crisis resources for launch jurisdictions.]**

### 33. Privacy

**Purpose.** To protect members' personal information within the community. How WiseWave Limited handles your data is governed by the Privacy Policy (`/legal/privacy`); this section is about how members must respect each other's privacy.

**Expected behaviour.** Keep what you learn about others private; ask before sharing anything about another member.

**Prohibited behaviour.** Publishing or threatening to publish another person's private information ("doxxing"); sharing others' images or messages without consent (see §§19, 34).

**Possible enforcement.** Content removal, restriction, suspension, or removal (§§42–44); referral where conduct may be unlawful (§47).

### 34. Recording Conversations

**Purpose.** To protect members from non-consensual capture and sharing of their communications.

**Expected behaviour.** Treat private conversations as private; obtain consent before recording or sharing them.

**Prohibited behaviour.** Recording, screenshotting, or sharing another member's private communications or images to harass, shame, blackmail, or expose them (§§19, 22, 33).

**Possible enforcement.** Content removal, restriction, suspension, or removal (§§42–44); referral to authorities where unlawful (§47).

### 35. AI Generated Content

**Purpose.** To preserve authenticity as synthetic media becomes common.

**Expected behaviour.** Represent yourself with genuine, unaltered images and honest information; do not pass off AI-generated personas as real people.

**Prohibited behaviour.** Using AI-generated faces or personas to create fake or deceptive profiles (§25); using AI tools to deceive, defraud, or harass.

**Possible enforcement.** Photo/identity review, restriction, suspension, or removal (§§43–44).

### 36. Deepfakes

**Purpose.** To prohibit synthetic media that impersonates or violates others.

**Expected behaviour.** Only share genuine media of yourself.

**Prohibited behaviour.** Creating or sharing deepfakes that impersonate a real person, or synthetic intimate imagery of any person without consent (a form of image-based abuse, §19); any synthetic sexual content involving minors is handled under §21.

**Possible enforcement.** Immediate removal, suspension, or permanent removal (§§43–44); referral to authorities (§47).

### 37. Manipulated Images

**Purpose.** To keep photos honest and prevent deceptive or abusive edits.

**Expected behaviour.** Use accurate images of yourself; minor, non-deceptive edits (such as cropping) are fine.

**Prohibited behaviour.** Materially misleading edits designed to deceive about identity or appearance, and any manipulated intimate imagery of others without consent (§§19, 36).

**Possible enforcement.** Photo review, content removal, restriction, or removal (§§42–44).

### 38. Reporting

**Purpose.** Reporting is how the community helps keep Tirvea safe. We rely on member reports alongside our own systems (§40).

**Expected behaviour.** Report content or conduct that breaches these Guidelines using the in-product reporting tools; report in good faith with accurate information. For copyright, security, or legal matters, use the dedicated routes in the Copyright Policy (`/legal/copyright`), Vulnerability Disclosure Policy (`/legal/vulnerability-disclosure`), and Law Enforcement Guidelines (`/legal/law-enforcement`).

**Prohibited behaviour.** Knowingly false, abusive, or bulk reports intended to harass another member or overwhelm moderation.

**Possible enforcement.** We assess valid reports and act under §§40–44; abusive reporting may itself be actioned. Reporters are protected; how report data is handled is described in the Privacy Policy (`/legal/privacy`).

### 39. Emergency Reporting

**Purpose.** To ensure urgent risks to life or safety are escalated quickly.

**Expected behaviour.** If someone is in immediate danger, contact your local emergency services first, then report to us so we can act (for example, preserve evidence and take enforcement action). Use emergency reporting for credible threats (§15), child-safety concerns (§21), and imminent harm.

**Prohibited behaviour.** Misusing emergency reporting for non-urgent matters.

**Possible enforcement.** Urgent reports may trigger **emergency suspension** of an account pending review (§43), evidence preservation, and referral to authorities (§47). Child-safety emergencies follow the Child Safety Policy (`/legal/child-safety`).

### 40. Moderation

**Purpose.** To explain, accurately, how we detect and act on breaches. Our approach combines automated systems and human judgement; the detail is in the Trust & Safety Policy (`/legal/trust-safety`) and AI Moderation Policy (`/legal/ai-moderation`).

**How moderation works.**
- **AI-assisted detection.** Automated systems help detect potential breaches at scale (for example, harmful content, scam patterns, and duplicate accounts). They **assist** rather than replace human decision-making.
- **Human review.** Consequential decisions are made or confirmed by trained human reviewers. **Automated systems do not impose bans on their own**: automation escalates at most to a temporary state (such as suspension) with an urgent case for a person to decide, so a permanent outcome always involves human review.
- **Identity and photo review.** Verification signals may trigger identity or photo review (`PHOTO_REVIEW_REQUIRED`), described in the Identity and Photo Verification Policies.
- **Risk scoring.** We assess the risk that an account or action is unsafe to prioritise review (see Privacy Policy §31, `/legal/privacy`).
- **Fraud and duplicate detection.** We detect fraud, scams, and duplicate or evasion accounts (§§23, 25).
- **Manual investigation.** Complex or serious cases are investigated by our Trust & Safety function.
- **Emergency suspension.** We may suspend an account immediately where necessary to prevent harm (§43).

**Statement of reasons.** For significant moderation decisions we provide a statement of reasons and information about how to appeal, consistent with our obligations under the Digital Services Act.

**Possible enforcement.** Any action in §§41–44. **[Legal review required before publication — confirm statement-of-reasons and notice-and-action drafting against the Digital Services Act.]**

### 41. Enforcement

**Purpose.** To explain how we choose enforcement actions. We apply a proportionate ladder based on the severity of the breach, intent, context, harm caused, and the member's history. The same conduct may attract different outcomes depending on these factors; the most serious breaches (such as child safety, §21) result in immediate permanent removal regardless of history.

**Expected behaviour.** Accept and comply with enforcement decisions; use appeals (§45) rather than evasion (§46) if you disagree.

**Prohibited behaviour.** Evading, circumventing, or retaliating against enforcement.

**Possible enforcement.** The graduated actions in §§42–44, and the account states described in the Account Suspension Policy (`/legal/account-suspension`).

### 42. Warnings

**Purpose.** For lower-severity or first-time breaches, a warning tells you what was wrong and how to correct it.

**Expected behaviour.** Read the warning, correct the behaviour, and continue within the Guidelines.

**Prohibited behaviour.** Repeating the conduct after a warning (see §46).

**Possible enforcement.** A warning may accompany content removal or a required correction; repetition escalates to restriction (§43).

### 43. Temporary Restrictions

**Purpose.** To limit harm while preserving the possibility of return, and to hold accounts pending review.

**Expected behaviour.** Comply with the restriction and, if you disagree, appeal (§45).

**Prohibited behaviour.** Creating new accounts or otherwise evading a restriction (§46).

**Possible enforcement.** Temporary states include: **feature limitation** (`LIMITED` — read access retained, outbound engagement such as likes and messages removed, tied to a violation that expires); **photo review** (`PHOTO_REVIEW_REQUIRED` — uploads blocked until review clears); and **suspension** (`SUSPENDED` — no app access, reversible and appealable), including **emergency suspension** where necessary to prevent harm. These states are described in the Account Suspension Policy (`/legal/account-suspension`).

### 44. Permanent Removal

**Purpose.** For the most serious or persistent breaches, permanent removal ends access to Tirvea.

**Expected behaviour.** Not applicable — this is the most serious outcome; an appeal route remains (§45).

**Prohibited behaviour.** Returning to the Service through new or duplicate accounts after removal (§46).

**Possible enforcement.** Permanent removal (`BANNED` — a terminal state) is used for severe breaches (such as child safety, §21; credible threats, §15; serious fraud, §23) or repeated serious violations. Removal remains **appealable** (§45). Data handling after removal follows the Data Retention Policy (`/legal/data-retention`) and Privacy Policy (`/legal/privacy`).

### 45. Appeals

**Purpose.** To give you a fair route to challenge a moderation or enforcement decision. Automated systems can make mistakes, and human decisions can be reconsidered.

**Expected behaviour.** If you believe a decision was wrong, submit an appeal in good faith through the process in the Appeals Policy (`/legal/appeals`), within any stated time limit.

**Prohibited behaviour.** Abusive, repetitive, or manifestly unfounded appeals; using appeals to harass.

**Possible enforcement.** On appeal we may uphold, reverse, or modify a decision.
