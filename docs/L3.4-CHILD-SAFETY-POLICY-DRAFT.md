## 1. Output - Repository audit summary

Findings from the implementation (see Output 5 for the consistency review):

**Implemented and relied on by this Policy:**
- **Adults-only access.** An explicit 18+ self-confirmation is required and recorded (with a hashed IP) before onboarding completes; the consent gate blocks progress until confirmed.
- **Verification.** Identity Verification (Stripe Identity) and Photo Verification (AWS Face Liveness and AWS Rekognition) support the confirmation that a member is a real adult.
- **User reporting.** Members can report and block. A report with the reason "underage" is created as a **MINOR_SAFETY** case at **CRITICAL** severity, ahead of ordinary reports.
- **Automated photo moderation.** Uploaded photos are automatically assessed by a moderation provider that returns a minor-risk signal. A minor-risk score at or above the configured threshold, or a "minors" label combined with adult content, is treated as **CRITICAL - minor safety first, always** and forces a policy-critical outcome and human review.
- **Human review and enforcement.** A moderation queue with severity-based prioritisation; account suspension and content removal; verification revocation; and appeals. Permanent bans are human-only. Actions are audited.

**Not implemented (implementation gaps - this Policy must not claim them):**
- **No perceptual-hash or known-database matching.** There is no PhotoDNA, CSAI Match, PDQ, or similar hash-matching against known child-sexual-abuse-material (CSAM) databases. Detection is limited to the automated photo-moderation classifier and to human review of reports.
- **No automated reporting to authorities or hotlines.** Reporting to competent authorities, hotlines, or bodies such as NCMEC or the IWF is an **organisational, manual process**, not an automated integration.
- **No automated evidence preservation orders.** Preservation for law enforcement is a manual process.
- **No automated chat or message scanning.** Automated moderation applies to **photos only**; messages rely on user reporting and human review.
- **No trusted-flagger or trusted-reporter programme** yet (a Digital Services Act consideration).
- **No age-estimation technology.** Age is self-declared and supported by verification; there is no automated age-estimation model.

Each gap is reflected in the relevant section below and carries a **[Legal review required before publication.]** flag where a legal obligation may exist that the implementation does not yet meet.

## 4. Output - Complete Child Safety Policy

> **Reading note.** Each operative section states its **Purpose**, **Scope**, **Trigger**, **Possible evidence**, **Decision maker**, **Possible outcomes**, and **Appeal availability**. Three principles run throughout: **child safety is the highest moderation priority and overrides platform convenience; reports involving children receive expedited review; and although automation may flag, detect, queue, restrict, and temporarily suspend, it may never permanently ban an account - permanent removal requires human review.**

### 1. Purpose

**Purpose.** To set out how WiseWave Limited prevents, detects, reviews, removes, reports, and responds to child sexual abuse material, child exploitation, grooming, trafficking, under-age accounts, and any content involving minors on Tirvea, an adults-only service.

**Scope.** The whole platform and every child-protection process.

**Trigger.** Not applicable (foundational section).

**Possible evidence.** Not applicable.

**Decision maker.** WiseWave Limited, through its Trust & Safety and child-safety functions.

**Possible outcomes.** A clear, authoritative child-safety framework with the highest enforcement priority.

**Appeal availability.** Enforcement decisions are appealable under the Appeals Policy (`/legal/appeals`), subject to the overriding legal and safety duties in this Policy.

### 2. Scope

**Purpose.** To state what this Policy covers.

**Scope.** All members, content, and interactions, and all age-assurance and child-protection processes. It applies alongside, and takes priority over, other enforcement policies where child safety is engaged.

**Trigger.** Not applicable.

**Possible evidence.** Not applicable.

**Decision maker.** Trust & Safety and child-safety functions.

**Possible outcomes.** Child-safety matters are handled under this Policy with the highest priority.

**Appeal availability.** As in §1.

### 3. Definitions

**Purpose.** To define child-safety terms. Conduct rules are defined in the Community Guidelines (`/legal/community-guidelines`) and Acceptable Use Policy (`/legal/acceptable-use`); enforcement states in the Account Suspension Policy (`/legal/account-suspension`); data terms in the Privacy Policy (`/legal/privacy`).

- **"Child" / "minor"** - a person under 18 years of age.
- **"CSAM"** - child sexual abuse material: any content that sexually depicts or exploits a minor, including synthetic or AI-generated material.
- **"Grooming"** - conduct intended to build a relationship with a minor to enable abuse or exploitation.
- **"Minor-safety case"** - the CRITICAL-severity case type raised when a child-safety concern is reported or detected.

**Trigger / evidence / decision maker / outcomes / appeal.** Not applicable (definitional).

### 4. Legal Framework

**Purpose.** To situate this Policy in law.

**Scope.** The legal regimes that shape child protection on the Service.

**Trigger.** Not applicable.

**Possible evidence.** Not applicable.

**Decision maker.** Legal and child-safety counsel.

**Possible outcomes.** This Policy is intended to operate consistently with applicable child-protection, online-safety, and data-protection law in the jurisdictions in which the Service operates, including the EU Digital Services Act and, on a future UK launch, the Online Safety Act. Specific statutory duties (including mandatory reporting) are jurisdiction-specific and are confirmed by counsel. **[Legal review required before publication - confirm the applicable child-protection and mandatory-reporting regimes for each launch jurisdiction.]**

**Appeal availability.** Not applicable (framework).

### 5. Children's Rights

**Purpose.** To affirm that children's safety and best interests come first.

**Scope.** All child-safety decisions.

**Trigger.** Not applicable.

**Possible evidence.** Not applicable.

**Decision maker.** Trust & Safety and child-safety functions.

**Possible outcomes.** Where child safety is engaged, the protection of children takes priority over platform convenience, member preferences, and ordinary process. This principle guides the interpretation of every section.

**Appeal availability.** Not applicable (principle).

### 6. Minimum Age Requirement

**Purpose.** To keep the Service strictly for adults.

**Scope.** All access and use.

**Trigger.** Registration and continued use.

**Possible evidence.** The recorded 18+ confirmation and any verification outcomes.

**Decision maker.** The onboarding gate and, for doubt or reports, the Trust & Safety function.

**Possible outcomes.** You must be at least 18 to create an account or use the Service. The onboarding gate blocks progress until an explicit 18+ confirmation is recorded. No person under 18 is permitted (§10).

**Appeal availability.** A removal for being under-age is appealable (§31), but access is not restored where the person is confirmed to be under 18.

### 7. Age Confirmation

**Purpose.** To require and record an explicit adult-age confirmation.

**Scope.** Onboarding and, where needed, re-confirmation.

**Trigger.** Account creation.

**Possible evidence.** The recorded confirmation event, stored with a hashed IP for audit; supporting verification outcomes (§8-§9).

**Decision maker.** The onboarding gate; Trust & Safety on doubt.

**Possible outcomes.** You confirm you are 18 or older before onboarding completes. This confirmation is self-declared; it is supported by, but is not on its own, formal age verification. Tirvea does not operate an automated age-estimation model. **[Legal review required before publication - confirm whether the launch jurisdictions require age assurance beyond self-declaration plus verification.]**

**Appeal availability.** As in §6.

### 8. Identity Verification

**Purpose.** To support adult-age assurance and reduce impersonation.

**Scope.** Members who undergo Identity Verification.

**Trigger.** A verification requirement, a feature gate, or a risk or safety signal.

**Possible evidence.** The verification outcome and an opaque provider reference; Tirvea does not store identity documents (see the Identity Verification Policy, `/legal/identity-verification`).

**Decision maker.** Provided by Stripe Identity; assessed by Trust & Safety where relevant.

**Possible outcomes.** A verified outcome supports adult-age assurance; a failed or fraudulent check can lead to restriction, suspension, or removal (Account Suspension Policy, `/legal/account-suspension`).

**Appeal availability.** Yes (§31).

### 9. Photo Verification

**Purpose.** To confirm a member is the real, live adult shown in their photos.

**Scope.** Members who undergo Photo Verification.

**Trigger.** A verification requirement or a risk or safety signal.

**Possible evidence.** Liveness and comparison outcomes and an opaque provider reference; no images or biometric templates are retained by Tirvea (see the Photo Verification Policy, `/legal/photo-verification`, and Biometric Information Policy, `/legal/biometric-data`).

**Decision maker.** Provided by AWS Face Liveness and AWS Rekognition; assessed by Trust & Safety where relevant.

**Possible outcomes.** Supports adult-age and identity assurance; failure can lead to review, restriction, or removal.

**Appeal availability.** Yes (§31).

### 10. Underage Accounts

**Purpose.** To remove accounts belonging to people under 18.

**Scope.** Any account believed or confirmed to belong to a minor.

**Trigger.** A report with the reason "underage", a detection signal, or information indicating a member is under 18.

**Possible evidence.** The report (raised as a CRITICAL minor-safety case), verification outcomes, and review findings.

**Decision maker.** Prioritised for human review; a human confirms removal.

**Possible outcomes.** A confirmed under-age account is removed. Related content is removed and, where a child-safety harm is present, handled under §12-§24. Access is not restored while the person is under 18.

**Appeal availability.** Yes (§31), subject to §6.

### 11. Age Misrepresentation

**Purpose.** To act against members who misrepresent their age.

**Scope.** False age information, in either direction, where it engages child safety.

**Trigger.** Evidence of age misrepresentation.

**Possible evidence.** Verification outcomes, reports, and review findings.

**Decision maker.** Trust & Safety, with human review for removal.

**Possible outcomes.** Removal of an under-age account (§10); restriction, suspension, or removal for adults who misrepresent age to enable harm (Account Suspension Policy, `/legal/account-suspension`).

**Appeal availability.** Yes (§31).

### 12. Child Sexual Abuse Material (CSAM)

**Purpose.** To enforce an absolute prohibition on CSAM.

**Scope.** Any content that sexually depicts or exploits a minor, real or synthetic.

**Trigger.** A report, or an automated photo-moderation minor-risk signal (a minor-risk score at or above the configured threshold, or a "minors" label with adult content), which is treated as CRITICAL.

**Possible evidence.** The moderation outcome or report, and preserved material handled under strict, purpose-limited procedures.

**Decision maker.** Automated systems flag and can restrict or suspend pending review; a human confirms and carries out removal and reporting. Automation does not permanently ban on its own.

**Possible outcomes.** Immediate content removal and account suspension pending review; permanent removal on human confirmation; preservation of relevant material; and reporting to the appropriate authorities. **Tirvea does not operate perceptual-hash or known-database matching (for example PhotoDNA or CSAI Match); detection is limited to the moderation classifier and human review.** Reporting to authorities and hotlines is an organisational, manual process. **[Legal review required before publication - confirm CSAM reporting obligations, designated bodies, and whether hash-matching or a reporting integration is legally required for the launch jurisdictions.]**

**Appeal availability.** An appeal route exists (§31), subject to the overriding legal and safety duties; content is not restored where restoration would be unlawful.

### 13. Child Exploitation

**Trigger.** A report or a detection signal.

**Possible evidence.** Reports, moderation outcomes, and review findings.

**Decision maker.** Prioritised for human review; a human confirms removal.

**Possible outcomes.** Removal, suspension, permanent removal (human), preservation, and reporting to authorities (§29).

**Appeal availability.** As in §12.

### 14. Sexualisation of Minors

**Possible evidence.** Moderation outcomes and reports.

**Decision maker.** Automated flag or human report; human review for consequential action.

**Possible outcomes.** Removal, restriction, suspension, or permanent removal (human), with escalation where the content is CSAM (§12).

**Appeal availability.** Yes (§31), subject to §12.
