## 1. Output - Repository audit

Findings from the implementation (see Output 5 for the review):

**Implemented / repository-backed (internal data sources exist):**
- **Reports.** Member reports are stored (`Report` model) and surfaced in the trust-and-safety admin views.
- **Moderation.** Moderation cases are tracked (`ModerationCase` model); content moderation runs through a provider abstraction with human review (`src/lib/services/moderation.ts`, `human-review-binding.ts`).
- **Appeals.** Appeals are stored and tracked (`Appeal` / `AppealEvent` models) with member safety notices on submission and decision (`src/lib/services/appeals.ts`).
- **Suspensions and bans.** Account status is a first-class field (`AccountStatus`: ACTIVE, SUSPENDED, BANNED, SHADOW_BANNED); enforcement outcomes are recorded.
- **AI / automated moderation.** Automated moderation is available through configurable providers (OpenAI, Google Vision, Hive, external) selected by configuration; it can **auto-suspend pending review at high confidence but never bans** - bans and permanent outcomes are **human decisions**.
- **Verification.** Verification operational metrics are exposed to operators (`/api/admin/verification-metrics`).
- **Audit.** An append-only administrative audit trail exists (`AdminLog`).

**Not implemented / not yet published / flagged:**
- **No published transparency report or figures.** There is **no public transparency report** and **no published statistics** in the codebase. This Policy therefore publishes **no numbers**; figures are **not yet published**. **[Legal review required before publication - decide the first reporting period and figures.]**
- **No transparency-metrics aggregation.** There is no built pipeline that aggregates reports, removals, appeals, suspensions, law-enforcement requests, child-safety actions, and AI-moderation outcomes into report-ready figures. **[Legal review required before publication - build the metrics aggregation.]**
- **No law-enforcement request register.** As noted in the Law Enforcement Guidelines, there is no dedicated request register, so authority-request figures are **not yet available**. **[Legal review required before publication.]**
- **No child-safety detection metrics.** There is no automated child-safety detection in the platform, so there are no automated-detection figures to report; manual actions would need to be compiled. **[Legal review required before publication.]**

## 4. Output - Complete Transparency Report Policy

> **Reading note.** This Policy explains **how** Tirvea reports on moderation and requests, and **what** we intend to publish. It contains **no figures**: transparency statistics are **not yet published**, and we will publish them once our reporting is established.

### 1. Purpose

**Purpose.** To explain how WiseWave Limited reports transparency information about content moderation, enforcement, appeals, authority requests, and child-safety and automated-moderation activity on Tirvea, and to state honestly what is and is not yet published.

### 2. Scope

**Purpose.** To state what this Policy covers. It covers transparency reporting for the Tirvea platform. It defines the categories we intend to publish and their data sources. It does not set the moderation rules themselves (Trust and Safety Policy) or the appeals process (Appeals Policy, `/legal/appeals`), and it does not itself contain the report; when figures are published, they will appear as a dated transparency report.

### 3. Our Commitment to Transparency

**Purpose.** To state our commitment. We believe members deserve to understand how the platform is moderated and how we respond to requests. We commit to publishing transparency information on a regular basis once our reporting is established, to describing our methodology, and to being clear about the **limits** of the data - including where a metric is **not yet available**.

### 4. Data Sources

**Purpose.** To identify where figures will come from. Our internal systems already record the underlying events that transparency figures are built from: member **reports**, **moderation cases**, **appeals** and their outcomes, account-status changes (**suspensions** and **bans**), verification operations, and an administrative **audit trail**. Transparency figures, when published, will be **aggregated** from these sources; the aggregation pipeline is being built (see §14).

### 5. Content Moderation

**Purpose.** To describe moderation reporting. We intend to report on **content moderation activity** - the volume of items actioned, the categories of policy involved, and the split between automated and human review. Moderation combines configurable automated providers with **human review** for consequential decisions. Figures are **not yet published**.

### 6. Reports

**Purpose.** To describe reporting on member reports. We intend to report the **number of reports** received from members, broken down by category where practicable, and how they were handled. The underlying reports are recorded internally; figures are **not yet published**.

### 7. Removals

**Purpose.** To describe reporting on removals. We intend to report **content removals and other content actions** taken as a result of moderation or reports, by category. Figures are **not yet published**.

### 8. Appeals

**Purpose.** To describe reporting on appeals. We intend to report the **number of appeals** submitted against enforcement decisions and their outcomes (for example upheld or reinstated). Appeals and their events are recorded internally; figures are **not yet published**.

### 9. Suspensions and Bans

**Purpose.** To describe reporting on enforcement outcomes. We intend to report **account suspensions and bans**, distinguishing temporary suspensions from permanent removals. Account status is recorded internally; figures are **not yet published**.

### 10. Law Enforcement Requests

**Purpose.** To describe reporting on authority requests. We intend to report the **number of law-enforcement and other authority requests** received, the number where some data was disclosed, and emergency requests, consistent with the Law Enforcement Guidelines (`/legal/law-enforcement`). There is **no dedicated request register yet**, so these figures are **not yet available**. **[Legal review required before publication - establish the register.]**

### 11. Child Safety

**Purpose.** To describe reporting on child safety. We intend to report on **child-safety actions** in a manner consistent with the **Child Safety Policy** (`/legal/child-safety`) and applicable law, and with appropriate care not to disclose information that could harm investigations or victims. The platform does **not** operate automated child-safety detection, so there are no automated-detection figures; any manual actions would be compiled for reporting. Figures are **not yet published**.

### 12. AI and Automated Moderation

**Purpose.** To describe reporting on automated moderation. We intend to report on **automated (AI-assisted) moderation**: the extent of automated review, and the principle that automation may flag or **temporarily suspend pending review** but does **not make permanent adverse decisions** - those are made by a **human**, consistent with the AI Moderation Policy (`/legal/ai-moderation`). Figures are **not yet published**.

### 13. Reporting Cadence and Format

**Purpose.** To describe how and how often we will report. We intend to publish a transparency report on a **regular cadence** (for example periodically), covering a defined reporting period, with methodology notes and category definitions. The specific cadence and first reporting period will be confirmed. **[Legal review required before publication - set the cadence and first period.]**

### 14. Current Status of Metrics

**Purpose.** To be completely clear about the present position. **No transparency figures are currently published.** The internal data sources exist, but the aggregation and reporting pipeline is **being built**, and some inputs (notably a law-enforcement request register and compiled child-safety actions) are **not yet in place**. We will publish figures once they can be produced **accurately**; we will not publish estimated or provisional numbers presented as measured. **[Legal review required before publication.]**

### 15. Policy Updates

**Purpose.** To explain changes. We may update this Policy as our reporting matures, including to begin publishing figures and to define categories and cadence. The current version and effective date are shown on this page.

### 16. Contact

**Purpose.** To tell you how to reach us.

- **Contracting entity:** WiseWave Limited (Company Number 762171)
- **Registered office:** 39 Cooley Park, Dundalk, Co. Louth, A91 AP2V, Ireland
- **Email:** info@tirvea.com

For moderation rules, see the Trust and Safety Policy; for appeals, the Appeals Policy (`/legal/appeals`); for automated moderation, the AI Moderation Policy (`/legal/ai-moderation`); for authority requests, the Law Enforcement Guidelines (`/legal/law-enforcement`).
