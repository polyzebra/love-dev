## 1. Output - Repository audit

Findings from the implementation (see Output 5 for the review):

**Implemented (repository-backed):**
- **Cookies actually set are strictly necessary only:** Supabase authentication cookies (names beginning `sb-`), a device security identifier (`tirvea_did`, up to 12 months), and Stripe cookies (`__stripe_mid`, `__stripe_sid`) that appear only during the payment/checkout flow.
- **Functional storage:** the appearance (theme) preference is stored in browser **local storage** (`tirvea:appearance`), not a cookie, and is first-party.
- **No analytics or marketing cookies** are set, and no analytics or advertising services are integrated.

**Not implemented (implementation gaps - this page must not claim them):**
- **No in-product cookie-preference tool or consent banner.** There is no consent-management interface; cookies are managed through browser controls.
- **The cookie consent-version framework is not wired.** A cookies consent version is declared in the legal registry, but the runtime consent record covers only Terms, Privacy, and Community Guidelines; there is no stored cookie-consent state.
- Because only strictly-necessary cookies are set, **no consent is currently required** to set the cookies in use; the consent controls below are forward-looking.

Each gap is reflected in the relevant section and carries a **[Legal review required before publication.]** flag.

## 4. Output - Complete Cookie Preferences Policy

> **Reading note.** This page explains how to manage cookies on Tirvea. Today, because only strictly-necessary cookies are set, the primary control is your browser; an in-product preference tool is being finalised and is flagged as such.

### 1. Purpose

**Purpose.** To explain how you can review and change your cookie choices on Tirvea, which categories of cookies we recognise, and how to manage them. The full list of cookies we use is in the Cookie Policy (`/legal/cookies`).

### 2. Scope

**Purpose.** To state what this page covers. It covers how to manage cookies and similar technologies. It does not restate the cookie inventory or durations (Cookie Policy, `/legal/cookies`) or how associated personal data is processed (Privacy Policy, `/legal/privacy`).

### 3. Cookie Categories

**Purpose.** To describe the categories we recognise: **Essential** (required to run the Service), **Functional** (remember your choices), **Analytics** (understand and improve the Service), and **Marketing** (advertising). Today we set only Essential cookies, and store one Functional preference in local storage; we do not use Analytics or Marketing cookies.

### 4. Essential Cookies

**Purpose.** Cookies without which the Service cannot function. These are always on and cannot be switched off through a preference tool; you can still block them in your browser, but the Service may then not work. On Tirvea they are: Supabase authentication cookies (keep you signed in), a device security identifier (`tirvea_did`, for fraud prevention), and Stripe cookies during checkout (payment-fraud prevention). These are exempt from consent under ePrivacy rules. See the Cookie Policy (`/legal/cookies`).

### 5. Functional Cookies

**Purpose.** Technologies that remember your choices. On Tirvea, your appearance (theme) preference is stored in your browser's **local storage** (not a cookie) and, when you are signed in, also saved to your account. We do not currently set other functional cookies. You can change the theme in the Service or clear your browser storage.

### 6. Analytics Cookies

**Purpose.** Cookies that would help us understand how the Service is used. **We do not currently use analytics cookies, and no analytics service is integrated.** If we introduce analytics in future, we will set non-essential analytics cookies **only with your prior consent**, and update this page and the Cookie Policy (`/legal/cookies`) first.

### 7. Marketing Cookies

**Purpose.** Cookies that would deliver or measure advertising. **We do not use marketing or advertising cookies, and we do not use social-media or advertising pixels.** If we ever introduce them, we will do so **only with your prior consent**.

### 8. Managing Preferences

**Purpose.** To explain how to manage your cookies today. Because only strictly-necessary cookies are set (and a functional preference in local storage), the primary way to manage cookies today is through your **browser controls** (§9). An in-product **Cookie Preferences** tool, which will let you accept or decline non-essential categories, is being finalised (§13). Essential cookies will always be on because the Service cannot function without them.

### 9. Browser Controls

**Purpose.** To explain device-level controls. Most browsers let you view, block, and delete cookies and clear local storage in their settings, and can alert you when a site sets a cookie. Blocking essential cookies will prevent parts of the Service from working - for example, you may be unable to stay signed in or complete a payment. Clearing the `tirvea:appearance` local-storage key resets your theme. Browser controls are provided by your browser, not by us.

### 10. Consent

**Purpose.** To explain the consent position. Non-essential cookies are set **only with your prior, specific, informed, and freely given consent**, in line with the GDPR and ePrivacy rules. **Today we set only strictly-necessary cookies, so no consent is currently required.** The consent controls described here are therefore forward-looking. **The cookie consent-version framework is declared but not yet wired into the runtime, so no cookie-consent state is currently stored (an implementation gap).** **[Legal review required before publication - confirm the consent design and wire the cookie consent-version before asserting a live consent gate.]**

### 11. Withdrawal of Consent

**Purpose.** To explain how to withdraw consent. Where you have consented to non-essential cookies, you may withdraw that consent at any time, as easily as you gave it. Today, because no non-essential cookies are set, withdrawal is achieved through your browser controls (§9); once the preference tool is available, you will be able to withdraw there. Withdrawal does not affect strictly-necessary cookies. Withdrawal of biometric (face) consent is a separate matter handled in privacy settings (Biometric Information Policy, `/legal/biometric-data`).

### 12. Third-Party Cookies

**Purpose.** To explain third-party cookies. The only third-party cookies are Stripe's, set during checkout for payment-fraud prevention. We do not permit third parties to set advertising or cross-site tracking cookies through the Service. See the Cookie Policy §16 (`/legal/cookies`).

### 13. Current Status of the Preference Tool

**Purpose.** To be transparent about the tool. **An in-product Cookie Preferences control is not yet available.** Until it is, manage cookies through your browser (§9). We will enable the preference tool before introducing any non-essential cookie, and we will update this page when it is live. **[Legal review required before publication - confirm the preference tool and the cookie consent wiring before publishing any statement that non-essential cookies are gated by a live tool.]**

### 14. Updates

**Purpose.** To explain how this page changes. We update this page to reflect changes in the cookies we use or the controls we offer - in particular before introducing any non-essential cookie or enabling the preference tool. We update the "Last Updated" date and, for material changes, seek renewed consent where required.

### 15. Contact

**Purpose.** To tell you how to reach us about cookies.

- **Data Controller:** WiseWave Limited (Company Number 762171)
- **Registered office:** 39 Cooley Park, Dundalk, Co. Louth, A91 AP2V, Ireland
- **Email:** info@tirvea.com

For the full cookie inventory, see the Cookie Policy (`/legal/cookies`); for your data rights, GDPR & Your Rights (`/legal/gdpr`).
