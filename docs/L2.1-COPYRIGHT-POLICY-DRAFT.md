## 1. Scope and purpose

**Purpose.** To set out how to report content on Tirvea that you believe infringes
your copyright or other intellectual-property rights, how WiseWave Limited responds,
and how an affected user can challenge a removal. Tirvea hosts user-submitted content
(profile photos and profile text). WiseWave Limited does not claim ownership of that
content; users are responsible for what they submit (Terms, §"User Content").

## 2. Respect for rights (your obligations)

**Respect for rights.** You may only submit content that you own or are otherwise
entitled to submit and that does not infringe any third party's rights. Submitting
infringing content breaches the Terms of Service and the Acceptable Use Policy and may
lead to content removal and account action under the Account Suspension Policy.

## 3. How to report infringement (notice and takedown)

**How to report.** Send a notice to **info@tirvea.com** (or use the in-app reporting
tools where available). To let us act, a notice should include:

1. Identification of the copyrighted work or right you claim is infringed.
2. Identification of the specific content on Tirvea (enough for us to locate it - for
   example, the profile and the specific photo or text).
3. Your contact details (name, email, and postal address where required).
4. A statement that you have a good-faith belief the use is not authorised by the
   rights holder, its agent, or the law.
5. A statement that the information in the notice is accurate and that you are the
   rights holder or authorised to act on their behalf.
6. Your electronic or physical signature.

**[Legal review required before publication.]** The exact statutory wording, sworn
statements, and any designated-agent details required in a given jurisdiction (for
example, the US DMCA §512(c)(3) elements) are confirmed by counsel before publication.

## 4. What we do with a valid notice

**Our response.** On receiving a notice that appears valid, WiseWave Limited will
review it and, where appropriate, **remove or disable access to the identified
content** and notify the affected user. WiseWave Limited may request further
information where a notice is incomplete. Removal decisions are content-moderation
actions and follow the same evidence-and-audit discipline as other Trust & Safety
actions (Trust & Safety Policy, AI Moderation Policy).

## 5. Counter-notice (challenging a removal)

**Counter-notice.** If your content was removed and you believe this was a mistake or
misidentification, you may submit a counter-notice to **info@tirvea.com** including:
the identified content and its former location, your contact details, and a statement
under penalty of perjury (where applicable) that you have a good-faith belief the
content was removed as a result of mistake or misidentification. Where the law
provides a restoration procedure, WiseWave Limited follows it.
**[Legal review required before publication.]** for the exact counter-notice
mechanics and any restoration timelines. Removals may also be challenged through the
Appeals Policy (`/legal/appeals`).

## 6. Repeat infringers

**Repeat-infringer policy.** WiseWave Limited maintains a policy of, in appropriate
circumstances, **suspending or terminating** the accounts of users who repeatedly
infringe intellectual-property rights. Account actions are applied and communicated
under the Account Suspension Policy (`/legal/account-suspension`) and may be appealed
under the Appeals Policy (`/legal/appeals`).

## 7. Trademarks and other IP

**Other rights.** This Policy applies to copyright and, with necessary changes, to
trademark and other intellectual-property complaints. Report them the same way
(§3). **[Legal review required before publication.]** for any trademark-specific process.

## 8. Misuse of this process

**Misuse.** Knowingly submitting a false notice or counter-notice may carry legal
liability and is itself a breach of the Acceptable Use Policy. WiseWave Limited may
decline to act on, and may take action in response to, abusive or bad-faith reports.
